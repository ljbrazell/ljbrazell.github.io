Design Critieria

Objective is to learn about if-else statements. Design a program to play the game Pig.

Design Constraints

Rules of Pig: Each turn, a player repeatedly rolls a die until either a 1 is rolled or the player holds and scores the sum of the rolls (i.e. the turn total). At any time during a player's turn, the player is faced with two options: Roll or Hold. Roll: If the player rolls a 1, the player scores nothing for this turn, and it becomes the next player's turn. If the player rolls a number 2 - 6, the number on the die is added to the player's turn total, and the player's turn continues. Hold: The playerâ€™s turn total is added to the player's overall score, and it becomes the next player's turn. The first player to score 100 or more points wins.

Procedure

When it starts, the program will set all variables but the turn to 0. Turn will be 1. When it is someone's turn, it'll roll the dice and and show what number it landed on.

When playing with two players, it'll do what the comments on the left say. If the 'When I receive Hold' is connected to the right set of blocks, it includes a third player.

This set of blocks is necessary if you want the computer to play.

As part of the rules, the winner is the player who gets to 100 or more points. The game will play until player 1 or 2 reaches 100.

When the hold button is clicked, it will broadcast Hold. Once Hold is received, it will move on to the next player.

When the button Roll is clicked, it will broadcast Roll. Once roll is received it will roll the dice and generate a new number.

Here is the final product. As you can see, there is a Hold and Roll button, as well as a dice. The boxes with numbers in the orange are the variables that are set in the block-code. They show/keep track of the scores and turn for you.
